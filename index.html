Frequently Asked Questions

What is HAWK-i CRCE?

HAWK-i CRCE is a platform for students and professionals to learn and participate in cybersecurity activities such as CTF challenges, hands-on experience with real-world bugs and vulnerabilities, and guidance from industry experts. It aims to promote understanding of cybersecurity in the digital era.

Why Join HAWK-i CRCE?

Joining HAWK-i CRCE can be beneficial for those interested in cybersecurity as it offers hands-on experience, guidance from experts, a supportive community, and the opportunity to learn and participate in cybersecurity activities such as CTF challenges. It aims to promote understanding of cybersecurity in the digital era.

What is SummerCon CRCE?

SummerCon CRCE is an annual cybersecurity conference held in Mumbai, India. It brings together experts and enthusiasts to share knowledge and discuss latest trends in computer security. The conference includes various talks, workshops, and serves as a platform to connect industry leaders with talented resources. It is considered as a flagship event in the cybersecurity industry and valued by the Fr.CRCE student community.

What is Bug Bounty?

A bug bounty is a program where companies and organizations offer rewards for finding and reporting security vulnerabilities in their software or systems. It is open to the public and the goal is to find and fix vulnerabilities before they can be exploited. It aims to improve the security of systems and protect customer's data by incentivizing security researchers, hackers, and enthusiasts to find and report bugs.

What is Pentesting?

Penetration testing (Pen testing) is the process of simulating a cyber attack to evaluate the security of a computer system, network, or web application. The main goal is to identify weaknesses in the security infrastructure and provide recommendations to protect against cyber attacks.

What is CTF?

CTF stands for Capture the Flag, a type of computer security competition where participants solve computer-based challenges to earn points. These challenges can be related to cryptography, web security, or network security. CTFs are a fun way to learn and practice cybersecurity skills in a competitive environment.
